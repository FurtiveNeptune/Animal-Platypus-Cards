Platypus

A platypus has the ability of electroreception, meaning they can locate their prey in part by detecting electric fields generated by muscular contractions.

  • Scientific Name: : Ornithorhynchus anatinus
  • Average Length: : 18.5 inches
  • Average Lifespan: : 17 years
  • Habitat: Bodies of freshwater

The body and the broad, flat tail of the platypus are covered with dense, brown fur that traps a layer of insulating air to keep the animal warm. The fur is waterproof, and the texture is akin to that of a mole. The platypus uses its tail for storage of fat reserves.
